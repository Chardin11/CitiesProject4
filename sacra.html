Topology

Sacramento's terrain is primarily flat grasslands

Sacramento sits near three fault zones. The Cleveland Hills, Sierra Nevada Fault, and San Joaquin faults

Sacramento is located near four active volcanos:

  • Mount Shasta
    approximently 220 miles north of Sacramento
  • Medicine Lake Volcano
    approximently 294 miles north of Sacramento
  • Lassen Peak
    approximently 162 miles north of Sacramento
  • Clear Lake
    approximently 123 miles northwest of Sacramento

Bedrock Age

Sacramento's bedrock is composed of crystalline rocks of Paleozoic and Mesozoic age to unconsolidated alluvium of recent age.

Mitigation Plans

Sacramento has many mitigation plans in place for many different scenarios. There is information about evacuations, earthquakes, floods, lighting and or thunder storms, and extreme heat.

Seismic Hazards

 For Seismic Hazards if you are outside, move away from buildings, street lights, utility wires, and overpasses. Once clear of these dangers, drop, cover, and stay where you are until the shaking stops.
 If you are inside, get under a sturdy table or desk and stay where you are until the shaking stops. Try and stay as low to the ground as possible. Cover your head and neck with your arms if possible. Stay clear of glass, windows, and light fixtures. Don't go outside until the shaking stops. If you smell gas or hear a hissing noise, leave immediately.
 If you are in a moving vehicle, stop as quickly and safely as possible in a clear location. Make sure you are clear of buildings, trees, overpasses, and utility wires. Stay inside your vehicle, with your seat belt fastened until the shaking stops.

Evacuations
  • Consider removing any hanging objects outside. Bring any objects not nailed down, inside.
  • Protect indoor items. For instance, in an event of flooding, move valuables upstairs.
  • Follow the recommended evacuation routes. Do not take shortcuts, as streets may be blocked.
  • Continue to monitor local media outlets for additional situational information.
Volcanic Hazards

For Volcanic Hazards go to nearest evacuation spot set in place by the city or county. Keep a look out for any warnings that might posted about possible volcano eruptions.

Floods
  • If flooding occurs, do not walk in water above your ankles and do not attempt to drive in flood waters. Just 6 inches of moving water can knock you down; and 2 feet of water can sweep your vehicle away.
  • Avoid underpasses, underground parking garages, or basements.
  • Fill your vehicle with gas in case an evacuation notice is issued.
  • Stay away from rising streams, creeks, and rivers.
